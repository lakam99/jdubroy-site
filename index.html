Business Intermediary Services

Business intermediary services involve acting between or amongst parties buying and selling a business, who typically know each other, to bring about a successful reconciliation of the differing economic and other interests of the parties.

The parties might be competitors, suppliers, co-shareholders, family members and/or employees, or most frequently, the management team of a company. Although the underlying process is invariably dynamic and the players and issues differ, the ultimate the form of a successful and effective reconciliation remains relatively constant from case-to-case.

As with business brokerage services, to provide business intermediary services in Ontario, one must be registered under the Real Estate and Business Brokers Act 2002, or be a practising attorney providing a legal service.

Business Valuation

Business valuation is a process of determining the economic value of ownership interest in a business. The process is also sometimes referred to as business appraisal, or company valuation or company appraisal. Business valuations are conducted for any number of reasons

including determining a price to offer to buy or to sell a business and to determine value in shareholder disputes, matrimonial breakdown, and in tax and/or succession planning.

The value to be determined, be it for instance fair market value or fair value, is largely a function of the purpose of the business or company valuation or appraisal.

The interest being valued might represent 100% of the ownership of a business or a company, as a whole, or it may constitute a lesser proportion such as in the case of a minority interest.
